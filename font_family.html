Mahatma Gandhi, born Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi on October 2, 1869, was a pivotal figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule. He was a lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance (satyagraha) to achieve political and social progress. Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolence inspired movements
